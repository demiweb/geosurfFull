What is AD Fraud and How Does It Work?

There are many methods by which Ad fraud is perpetrated. A botnet is a popular form of Ad fraud. The Fraudsters operate from a group of computers that are hacked and infected by malware. This malware interferes with browsers and generates traffic for fake websites created by hackers. These fake sites are not visited by actual people, and yet these sites are generating the false impression of huge human traffic, attracting Ad Servers to throw Ads onto the fraudsters.

Due to these successful Ad Fraud attacks, hackers steal advertisements from Ad Servers generating more revenue for themselves. Advertisers have to bear the brunt of losses due to this fraud by performing fake clicks on Ads. Last year it was estimated that Russian hackers earned up to 3M to 5M USD a day by doing fake clicks on video Ads.
